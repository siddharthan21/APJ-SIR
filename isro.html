Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, also known as the "Missile Man of India," played a significant role in India's space program, specifically with ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation). While he is more renowned for his contributions to India's missile program, his association with ISRO was also notable. Here are some key aspects of his involvement with ISRO: 1. Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV): Dr. Kalam was involved in the early development of India's Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV), which was used for launching satellites into space. The successful launch of Rohini Satellite with SLV-3 in 1980 marked a milestone for ISRO, and Dr. Kalam's contributions to this project were significant. 2. Satellite Technology: Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for using satellite technology for various applications, including communication, remote sensing, and weather forecasting. He emphasized the importance of satellite technology for national development. 3. Promotion of Space Technology: As the President of India from 2002 to 2007, Dr. Kalam continued to promote space technology and encourage ISRO's initiatives. He used his position to inspire and support India's space scientists and engineers. 4. Vision for Space Exploration: Dr. Kalam had a vision for India to become a leader in space exploration. He often spoke about the need for India to explore outer space and conduct planetary missions, including missions to the Moon and Mars. His vision laid the foundation for future space exploration missions by ISRO. While Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's primary expertise and fame were in missile technology and defense, his support and encouragement for space research and ISRO's endeavors played a significant role in advancing India's capabilities in space technology and exploration. His legacy continues to inspire scientists and researchers in the field of space science and technology in India.
