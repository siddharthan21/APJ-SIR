Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam made significant contributions to India's nuclear program throughout his career. Here are some key aspects of his involvement in India's nuclear endeavors: 1. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's second nuclear tests, codenamed "Operation Shakti," which took place in May 1998 at the Pokhran Test Range in Rajasthan. He was the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Government of India and the head of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) at that time. Under his leadership, India successfully conducted a series of nuclear tests, including underground tests, which made India a declared nuclear weapon state. 2. Nuclear Weapons Development: Dr. Kalam's expertise in missile technology was closely linked to India's nuclear weapons program. He contributed to the development of delivery systems for nuclear warheads, such as ballistic missiles, and played a significant role in integrating these systems with India's nuclear capabilities. 3. Missile Development: Dr. Kalam is widely known for his contributions to India's missile development programs, including the Agni series of ballistic missiles. These missiles are capable of carrying nuclear warheads, and Dr. Kalam's leadership at DRDO helped India establish a credible deterrent capability. 4. Advocacy for Peaceful Uses: While Dr. Kalam was deeply involved in India's nuclear weapons program, he also advocated for the peaceful uses of nuclear technology. He believed that nuclear energy could play a crucial role in India's energy security and development. He emphasized the importance of using nuclear technology for peaceful purposes, such as power generation and medical applications. 5. International Engagement: Dr. Kalam represented India on the international stage and engaged in discussions related to nuclear disarmament and non-proliferation. He stressed India's commitment to a no-first-use (NFU) policy, which means that India would not use nuclear weapons first in any conflict. It's important to note that Dr. Kalam's contributions to India's nuclear program were as a scientist and technocrat, and he faithfully served the policies and decisions of the Indian government. His work in the fields of missile technology and nuclear technology played a crucial role in strengthening India's defense capabilities.
